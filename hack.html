Gradience is a hack!

What makes Gradience a hack?

Gradience overrides libadwaita's Named Colors.

Gradience replaces libadwaita's Named Colors with its own custom values. This can cause issues such as invisible text or broken palettes.

Gradience is not supported by libadwaita.

Gradience isn't officially supported by the GNOME team. If significant changes are made to libadwaita upstream, this can cause Gradience to stop working correctly.

Gradience can break custom UI.

Gradience can break some apps which rely on setting their own colors and styles to properly display user interface elements.

Gradience is not against stopthemingmy.app

The Gradience team does not oppose the message laid out by stopthemingmy.app and the developers of libadwaita. Gradience is a tool for tinkerers that wish to theme their desktops to their liking; not a tool for distributions to ship hacky style changes to users by default. The Gradience team believes in the importance of a stable and unified look due to the benefits it brings to the design of apps.
